## Behold, the forbidden knowledge. Browse at your own risk..

<CardGrid>

<Card title="For Adventurers" icon="star">
  Consult the records between sessions to recall what truly happened—not just
  the drunken tavern retelling. The Session Ledger preserves every battle,
  bargain, and bad decision in order.
</Card>

<Card title="For the Observer" icon="rocket">
  The Library is maintained entirely in Obsidian. As you write in your vault,
  the archives are updated through Git and rebuilt automatically, keeping the
  public record in sync with your private notes.
</Card>

<Card title="The Session Ledger" icon="list-format">
  Visit the <a href="/sessions/">Sessions</a> shelves for a
  chronological record of the campaign. Each entry can reference characters,
  locations, and lore—forming a web of cross-linked knowledge.
</Card>

<Card title="The Lore Stacks" icon="open-book">
  The <a href="/lore/">Lore</a> stacks contain histories,
  prophecies, pantheons, factions, and cosmology. Ideal for late-night
  theory-crafting and “wait, wasn’t there a prophecy about this?” moments.
</Card>

<Card title="The Character Index" icon="comment-alt">
  Browse the <a href="/characters/">Character Index</a> to
  remember who you’ve met, who you’ve wronged, and who is smiling a little too
  kindly.
</Card>

<Card title="The Atlas of Omain" icon="puzzle">
  The <a href="/locations/">Locations</a> section charts cities,
  ruins, taverns, and strange places. Each map entry can link to sessions and
  characters tied to that place.
</Card>

<Card title="How the Library Endures" icon="seti:html">
  Behind the shelves is a static Starlight build: markdown in, archive out. As
  long as the Obsidian vault survives, so does the Grand Library.
</Card>

<Card title="New Wings & Collections" icon="add-document">
  Need a bestiary, item compendium, or quest log? Add new markdown under
  <code>src\content\docs</code> and configure a new section, another wing in the
  Archives.
</Card>

</CardGrid>
